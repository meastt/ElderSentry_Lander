Frequently Asked Questions

We're currently in development and planning a beta launch in 2026. Join our waitlist to be notified when we open beta testing to select families. Early waitlist members will get priority access and special launch pricing.
Our wearable device uses advanced accelerometer and gyroscope sensors to detect sudden movements consistent with falls. When a fall is detected, the system immediately sends alerts to all designated family members via SMS and app notifications, and can initiate two-way voice communication.
Family members can use our mobile app on iOS (iPhone 8 and newer) or Android (version 9.0 and up) devices. The senior wears our custom monitoring device—no smartphone required. Setup takes less than 5 minutes with our guided app instructions.
We're planning a one-time device purchase with no monthly subscription fees. All features—including fall detection, medication reminders, family notifications, and activity monitoring—will be included. Final pricing will be announced closer to launch.
Absolutely. We use end-to-end encryption for all data transmission, and all health information is stored securely on your family's devices with encrypted cloud backup. We never sell data to third parties, and you have complete control over who can access your loved one's information. Read our full Privacy Policy for details.
Yes! You can add unlimited family members and caregivers to receive alerts. Each person can customize their notification preferences (SMS, app, email) and set quiet hours. You can also designate primary and secondary contacts for different types of alerts.
The device has a 7-day battery life under normal use. When the battery drops below 20%, all family members receive low-battery alerts. The device continues working until completely drained, giving plenty of time to charge. We also send weekly charging reminders via the app.
Yes! The device has built-in cellular connectivity, so fall detection and alerts work anywhere with cell coverage—at home, in the yard, at the store, or visiting friends. GPS location is shared with family members during alerts so they know exactly where help is needed.
