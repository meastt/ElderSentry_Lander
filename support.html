Frequently Asked Questions

How does Elder Sentry work?

Simply tap the share button on any suspicious text or email, select Elder Sentry, and get an instant analysis. Our AI examines the message and tells you if it's safe or potentially a scam, with clear explanations in plain language.

Is my data private?

Yes, absolutely. We analyze messages only to detect scams. We don't store your personal information or share your data with anyone. Your messages stay private.

How much does it cost?

Elder Sentry offers free daily scans to get you started. Premium plans with unlimited checks are available for those who need them. No hidden fees or subscriptions required for basic use.

What types of scams can Elder Sentry detect?

Elder Sentry detects a wide range of scams including phishing emails, fake bank alerts, lottery scams, tech support fraud, IRS/Social Security imposters, family emergency scams, package delivery frauds, and Medicare scams.

Do I need to be tech-savvy to use it?

Not at all. Elder Sentry was designed to be simple—just one tap and you get a clear answer in plain language. No technical knowledge required.

Can I use it for both texts and emails?

Yes! Elder Sentry works with both text messages and emails on your iPhone. Just use the share button from either app.

What if I've already fallen for a scam?

If you believe you've been scammed, contact us immediately at support@eldersentry.com. We can provide guidance on next steps, including reporting to authorities and protecting your accounts.

What devices is Elder Sentry compatible with?

Elder Sentry is available for iPhone and iPad running iOS 15.0 or later. Download it from the App Store.

How accurate is the scam detection?

Our AI is trained on millions of scam patterns and is constantly updated with new threats. While no system is 100% perfect, Elder Sentry provides highly accurate detection with clear explanations so you can make informed decisions.

Can I report a scam through the app?

Yes! Elder Sentry provides resources and guidance for reporting scams to the appropriate authorities, including the FTC, FBI, and other agencies.
